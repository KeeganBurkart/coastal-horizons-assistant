# Services Offered by Coastal Horizons

## Substance Use, Mental Health & Integrated Care Services
Continuum of prevention, outpatient treatment, and health-related services in eastern NC — primary emphasis in New Hanover, Pender, and Brunswick counties.
Overview page: https://coastalhorizons.org/services/substance-use-mental-health/services-overview/

### Adult Outpatient Treatment
- Individual, group, and family counseling for substance use and/or mental health disorders
- Auxiliary medical and psychiatric services; co-located behavioral health at primary care facilities
- Substance Abuse Intensive Outpatient Program (SAIOP / IOP) — 12-week program of group, individual, and family therapy plus case management (New Hanover, Brunswick, Pender)
- Substance Abuse Comprehensive Outpatient Treatment (SACOT) — higher-level structured addiction treatment (Brunswick, Pender)
- DWI assessments and full array of DWI services: https://coastalhorizons.org/services/substance-use-mental-health/dwi-services/
- Clinica Latina — specialized outpatient services in Spanish (609 Shipyard Blvd., Ste. 106, Wilmington; 910-769-1201)

### Medication Assisted Treatment for Opioid Use Disorder (MOUD)
- Opioid Treatment Program (OTP) — methadone & buprenorphine (New Hanover, Brunswick, Pender)
- Office-Based Buprenorphine (New Hanover, Pender, Brunswick)
- Page: https://coastalhorizons.org/services/substance-use-mental-health/medication-assisted-opioid-treatment-methadone-buprenorphine/

### Adult Residential & Community Services
- HARRTS — intensive treatment integrated with a network of recovery homes
- HUD Horizons Housing Program (appointments: 910-444-0621)
- PATH — street outreach/case management for people with severe and persistent mental illness experiencing chronic homelessness
- Quick Response Team (QRT, Wilmington) — peer support specialists and therapists engaging overdose survivors and those at high risk (910-833-2052)
- Post Overdose Response Team
- Jail Diversion Program
- Flourishing Families (formerly Pregnant & Postpartum Women's program) — Whiteville, 910-524-8183

### Child, Adolescent & Family Services (Community Based Family Services, CBFS)
- Intensive In-Home Services (serious emotional/behavioral/substance use disorders; 7 counties)
- Child First — infant/young child mental health treatment & prevention, trauma-focused (21 counties)
- HOMEBUILDERS® — intensive family preservation (42 counties)
- Incredible Years — parenting education and support program
- Family Centered Treatment — evidence-based home-based family therapy
- Day Treatment — Brunswick, Pender (PILA), Lake Forest Academy K-8 (New Hanover), Bladen
- Child mental health therapy: outpatient individual/family therapy, medical & psychiatric services, school-based behavioral health
- Programs for adjudicated youth and their families

### Prevention & Outdoor Adventure
- Community prevention of alcohol, tobacco, and other drug use
- Challenge Course / Alpine Tower (330B Shipyard Blvd., Wilmington; 910-392-7306)

## Health & Wellness (Integrated Care)
- Horizons Health — primary care/integrated care for adults (613 Shipyard Blvd., Wilmington; 910-202-3860)
- WHAT — Wilmington Health Access for Teens (1414 Medical Center Dr., Wilmington; 910-790-9949)
- School-Based Health Centers at Ashley, Hoggard, Laney, and New Hanover high schools
- HIV & Hepatitis outreach, testing, education, and early-intervention support
- Peer support, care management, hospital care integration (NHRMC partnership)
- Note: Coastal Horizons does not offer a Safe Syringe Program; NC Harm Reduction Coalition and Port City Harm Reduction do.
