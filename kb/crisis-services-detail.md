## Rape Crisis Center

**In crisis due to sexual assault or abuse? Call 910-392-7460** (24-hour crisis hotline) or email supportrcc@coastalhorizons.org to speak with a counselor. Servicios disponibles para personas que hablan español.

- The only stand-alone rape crisis center in Southeastern North Carolina.
- Serves New Hanover, Brunswick, and Pender Counties.
- All services are free and confidential, provided to all victims of sexual violence in the community by staff, interns, and volunteers.

Services:
- 24-hour crisis response to victims via the hotline and in person at hospital emergency rooms.
- Criminal justice advocacy for sexual assault victims: support through law enforcement interviews, meetings with the district attorney's office, and throughout any criminal or civil court proceeding.
- Free individual therapy and weekly support groups for sexual violence survivors who would not otherwise have access (e.g., uninsured or high co-pay).
- Assistance with filing protective orders and Crime Victim's Compensation.
- All therapy services are facilitated by licensed clinicians using evidence-based treatments: Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) and Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TFCBT).

Community outreach: Awareness and Education Presentations and Educational Programming, available virtually. To schedule a virtual presentation or for community resources, call the office line at 910-392-7185.

Dedicated website: coastalhorizonsrcc.org — full list of services, resources for victims and their loved ones, educational resources, and volunteer information.

Source: https://coastalhorizons.org/crisis-intervention/rape-crisis-center/

## Open House Youth Shelter and Residential Services

**For more information call 910-392-7408.** Referrals are accepted 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

- Serves displaced, abused, neglected, and vulnerable youth.
- Children and adolescents ages 6–18 who need a safe place to stay can receive shelter in the 24-hour, 9-bed facility.
- Beyond food, clothing, and shelter: counseling services, vocational/educational opportunities, life skill building, and recreational activities.
- All services are confidential and provided at no cost to youth and their families; services continue for three months after discharge to support the family reunification plan.
- Referrals accepted 24/7 from local agencies (Department of Social Services, law enforcement, Juvenile Justice, school personnel, and other youth-serving organizations and professionals). Self-referrals and referrals from parents/legal guardians are also accepted.
- Licensed biennially through the North Carolina Division of Social Services for temporary Emergency Foster Care placements and long-term Residential/Foster Care services; this dual licensure allows services to be adapted to each youth's needs.
- More info: www.supportopenhouseservices.org or the Open House Youth Shelter Facebook page.

Source: https://coastalhorizons.org/crisis-intervention/open-house/

## Witnessing an Opioid Overdose — Emergency Steps (SAMHSA guidance)

**Call 911 immediately.** Then, while waiting for emergency responders:

1. Administer naloxone (Narcan) if it is available and you know how to use it.
2. Try to keep the person awake and breathing.
3. Lay the person on their side (recovery position) to prevent choking.
4. Stay with the person until emergency workers arrive.

After the emergency: Coastal Horizons' Quick Response Team (QRT, Wilmington — 910-833-2052) engages overdose survivors and people at high risk to connect them with peer support and treatment.

Source: SAMHSA Opioid Overdose toolkit (verified 2026-06-11); QRT from coastalhorizons.org
